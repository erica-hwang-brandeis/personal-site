Part 1: Review documentation for the Open Source Hardware

I found that the video was helpful in providing a visual of the assembly. However, the video was a bit confusing and hard to follow along, so I ended up relying more on the PDF instructions. The labeling of the files was not intuitive, so I had to open each one and skim them in order to know which I needed to use.

View e-NABLE page here

Part 2: 3D Print & assemble the model

Using the Phoenix Hand model, we scaled it to 150% in PrusaSlicer. The "thermo gaunlet" print only printed half way for me, so I had to restart that print. Assembling the hand was less complicated than I expected, since most of the steps were just putting in pegs to connect the finger joints to the hand. The most challenging step in the assembly process was the stringing, since the loops and holes were a bit hard to differentiate. When the joints weren't bending like they were supposed to, we realized we were using the wrong string so had to undo and restart the stringing.

Part 3: Extend, critique & reflect

After building the Phoenix Hand, I started thinking about how it could be used for more specific tasks. One idea I had was to add a small attachment to the palm that could hold different types of grips—like a utensil holder or something with better traction for holding bottles. I sketched the idea and made a quick CAD model in Fusion 360, just to see how it might look. One of the biggest challenges during this project was following the different sets of documentation. Some parts were really helpful, but other times I had to search multiple pages just to find what I needed. It reminded me of something Don Norman said about how good design should make things obvious and easy to use. The process would’ve been smoother if the instructions were more consistent and organized. Overall, I thought it was really cool to see how open-source design can make such a big impact. It also made me realize how important clear instructions are, especially for projects like this where the goal is to help people.
